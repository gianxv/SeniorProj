Finding Home as Filipino Immigrants in Hawai‘i

In Search of Nurture In Such A New Environment

Moving to Hawaii from the Philippines wasn’t just a change of place—it was a whole new world. I remember the first time I walked into a classroom and didn’t understand a word anyone was saying. I felt small, unsure, and very far from home.

But slowly, things changed.

We learned to navigate English, to find comfort in local food that reminded us of home while also falling in love with loco moco and malasadas. We discovered that the Filipino community in Hawai‘i is strong, loud, and filled with heart.

Still, it wasn’t easy. Some of us were bullied. Some of us felt ashamed of our accents or our parents’ jobs. We wrestled with guilt—between wanting to fit in and wanting to stay true to where we came from.

But through the struggle came growth. We found mentors. We made friends who felt like family. We realized that home isn’t always a place—it’s the people who stand with you, who speak your language when no one else does, who understand why you still cry when you hear your mom singing a lullaby when you were young. This is our story. One of loss, yes—but also of finding new roots and building a home where we can finally breathe.

Embracing Two Worlds

In Search of Nurture In Such A New Environment

Every Christmas, the balikbayan boxes would arrive—filled with canned goods, toiletries, and clothes. To many, they were just packages. To us, they were reminders of a life we left behind and a culture we’re still learning how to hold on to.

Growing up in Hawai‘i as a young Filipino immigrant meant constantly living in two worlds. At home, we ate sinigang and spoke Tagalog. Outside, we were taught to blend in, speak English, and not cause trouble.

We faced language barriers and cultural misunderstandings. We were told to “be American,” but sometimes we didn’t know what that meant. We were told to be proud of being Filipino, but we didn’t always feel like we belonged there either.

We’re still learning what it means to belong. But we’re no longer afraid to take up space—to be both Filipino and local, to be proud of where we come from and where we are now.
